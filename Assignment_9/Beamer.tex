\section{Problem}
Find QR decomposition of \myvec{2&3\\3&-4}
\section{Solution}
Let $\vec{a}$ and $\vec{b}$ be transpose of column vectors of the given matrix.
\begin{align}
\vec{a}&=\myvec{2\\3}\\
\vec{b}&=\myvec{3\\-4}
\end{align}
The column vectors can be expressed as follows,
\begin{align}
\vec{a}&=k_1\vec{u}_1\label{eqQR1}\\
\vec{b}&=r_1\vec{u}_1+k_2\vec{u}_2\label{eqQR2}
\end{align}
Here, 
\begin{align}
k_1 &= \norm{\vec{a}}\label{eq1}\\
\vec{u}_1 &= \frac{\vec{a}}{k_1}\\
r_1 &= \frac{\vec{u}_1^T\vec{b}}{\norm{\vec{u}_1}^2}\\
\vec{u}_2 &= \frac{\vec{b} - r_1 \vec{u}_1}{\norm{\vec{b} - r_1 \vec{u}_1}}\\
k_2 &= {\vec{u}_2^T\vec{b}}\label{eq2}
\end{align}
The \eqref{eqQR1} and \eqref{eqQR2} can be written as, 
\begin{align}
\myvec{\vec{a} & \vec{b}} &= \myvec{\vec{u}_1 & \vec{u}_2}\myvec{k_1 & r_1 \\ 0 & k_2}\\
\myvec{\vec{a} & \vec{b}} &= \vec{Q}\vec{R}\label{QRMain}
\end{align}
Now, $\vec{R}$ is an upper triangular matrix and also,
\begin{align}
\vec{Q}^T\vec{Q}=\vec{I}
\end{align}
Now using equations \eqref{eq1} to \eqref{eq2} we get, 
\begin{align}
k_1 &= \sqrt{2^2+3^2} = \sqrt{13}\label{eqval1}\\ 
\vec{u}_1 &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{13}}\myvec{2\\3}\\
r_1 &= \myvec{\frac{2}{\sqrt{13}}&\frac{3}{\sqrt{13}}}\myvec{3\\-4} = -\frac{6}{\sqrt{13}}\\ 
\vec{u}_2 &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{13}}\myvec{3\\-2}\\
k_2 &= \myvec{\frac{3}{\sqrt{13}}&-\frac{2}{\sqrt{13}}}\myvec{3\\-4} = \frac{17}{\sqrt{13}}\label{eqval2} 
\end{align}
Thus putting the values from \eqref{eqval1} to \eqref{eqval2} in \eqref{QRMain} we obtain QR decomposition,
\begin{align}
\myvec{2&3\\3&-4}=\myvec{\frac{2}{\sqrt{13}}&\frac{3}{\sqrt{13}}\\\frac{3}{\sqrt{13}}&-\frac{2}{\sqrt{13}}}\myvec{\sqrt{13}&-\frac{6}{\sqrt{13}}\\0&\frac{17}{\sqrt{13}}}
\end{align}
